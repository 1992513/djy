<tr><td VALIGN=TOP width="626"><h2 align=center>流行美语 第201课</h2>
<h6></h6>
<hr>
<p>【大纪元11月12日讯】<--ads--><br /><center><ahref=https://www.freexinwen.com/chinese/eng/pa/download.asp?file=/chinese/s_pa/pa201.mp3>(MP3下载)</a></center><center><ahref=https://www.freexinwen.com/chinese/eng/pa/download.asp?file=/chinese/s_pa/pa201.rm>(Real下载)</a></center></p>
<p>李华今天在Larry家玩电子游戏，给Larry打败。 李华今天会学到两个常用语：own someone和goner。</p>
<p>[Sound of video games]</p>
<p>LH: 哎，我又输了。这个游戏真不怎么样。</p>
<p>LL: Ha! I own you Li Hua! That&#8217;s five games to zero! I totally own you!</p>
<p>LH: Larry，你那么激动干什么呀！你说什么？“I own you ”，你又不欠我什么呀！</p>
<p>LL: No, not owe, I said own! To &#8220;own&#8221; someone means to overwhelmingly defeat that person at a game or contest, or just to be a lot better than someone else at something.</p>
<p>LH: 噢，to own someone就是玩游戏或比赛的时候把某人打败，或者在某个方面比对方强得多。你是说，我们玩电子游戏，你把我打败了。这有什么了不起！ 上次打乒乓球我把你打的落花流水，你记得吗？</p>
<p>LL: Yeah, I know, I know, the last time we played, you beat me 21 to zero. You totally own me at ping-pong. But that&#8217;s just because in the U.S. we don&#8217;t think of ping-pong as a real sport.</p>
<p>LH: 什么？你们美国人不把乒乓看作是真正的体育？That&#8217;s baloney! 好吧，打保龄球在美国是很流行的，是不是！你还记得有一次我得了230分， 你连100分都不到。Larry，这你该怎么说呢？</p>
<p>LL: Right, right, you definitely own me at bowling. That was pretty embarrassing. Of course, I had given blood that day, and&#8230;</p>
<p>LH: 嗨，别再找理由了。你自己觉得不好意思就行！ 你呀， 除了打电子游戏以外，我看你什么都玩不过我。</p>
<p>LL: Oh yeah? What about Scrabble? I own you at Scrabble any day!</p>
<p>LH: 拼字游戏？ 当然了， 你是土生土长的美国人，英语是你的母语呀，如果连拼字你都比我差，那也太不好意思了吧。</p>
<p>LL: In spite of what you believe, I also own the tennis court. I&#8217;m the best player on the tennis court. I can beat anyone else who comes onto the court.</p>
<p>LH: 我承认你的网球的确打得不错，到目前为止，谁也打不过你，不过那是因为你还没有遇到好的对手。</p>
<p>LL: Come on, Li Hua, it&#8217;s silly to argue like that. Isn&#8217;t it obvious that everyone can own someone in certain fields, but no one can own everyone in everything.</p>
<p>LH：这话不错。每个人都有比别人强的地方，但是不能什么都比人强。</p>
<p>******</p>
<p>LH: 好了， Larry, 电子游戏打得差不多了， 我们出去吃点东西吧。</p>
<p>LL: Wait, just let me finish this round [sound of TV popping and shutting off] Whoa, the TV died. Oh well, I guess it&#8217;s a goner now.</p>
<p>LH: 电视坏了， 你说电视是什么？ Goner ？</p>
<p>LL: I said, &#8220;It&#8217;s a goner&#8221;. That means that the TV is &#8220;gone&#8221;. It&#8217;s dead, it cannot be used again or brought back to life.</p>
<p>LH: 我明白了，你说 &#8220;the TV is a goner&#8221; 意思是这电视机坏了，没救了。你怎么知道呀？也许还可以修呢！</p>
<p>LL: I&#8217;v already fixed it once, I&#8217;m pretty sure it&#8217;s a goner this time.</p>
<p>LH: 原来你已经修过了，那看来是完蛋了。 Larry, 你看墙角的那盆花都枯黄了，看来是没救了。It is a goner。</p>
<p>LL: Oh man, I can&#8217;t believe I let that poor plant die. Yeah, it&#8217;s definitely a goner. Just like your goldfish Shan-shan.</p>
<p>LH: 你可别提我的金鱼， 一提我就难过。 Larry，你不觉得用goner来形容心爱得宠物或是人，有点不太合适吗？</p>
<p>LL: I&#8217;m sorry. You&#8217;re right, it is very insensitive to say someone&#8217;s pet, or loved one is &#8220;a goner&#8221;.</p>
<p>LH: 没错，我也觉得把别人心爱的东西或喜欢的人称为goner似乎不太礼貌，对不对？</p>
<p>LL: You&#8217;re absolutely right. Li Hua, do you remember my brother Jack?</p>
<p>LH: Jack? 就是那个不想上大学的弟弟吗？</p>
<p>LL: Yes. Since he didn&#8217;t have a college education, my parents said that he&#8217;s a goner in the job market for sure.</p>
<p>LH: 你爸爸妈妈认为你弟弟没有大学学历，要找工作是没有希望的。我看不一定吧。微软的创办人Bill Gates也没念完大学，可他现在是世界上最富有的人士之一。说不定Jack将来会很有出息呢。</p>
<p>今天李华学到了两个常用语。一个是own Someone，意思是在游戏和比赛中击败某人，或是在某个方面比别人强。李华学到的另一个常用语是goner意思是完了，没希望了。<font color=#ffffff>(https://www.dajiyuan.com)</font></p>
