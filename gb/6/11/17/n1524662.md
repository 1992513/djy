<tr><td VALIGN=TOP width="626"><h2 align=center>流行美语 第202课</h2>
<h6></h6>
<hr>
<p>【大纪元11月17日讯】<--ads--><br /><center><ahref=http://www.freexinwen.com/chinese/eng/pa/download.asp?file=/chinese/s_pa/pa202.mp3>(MP3下载)</a></center><center><ahref=http://www.freexinwen.com/chinese/eng/pa/download.asp?file=/chinese/s_pa/pa202.rm>(Real下载)</a></center><br />　</p>
<p>李华需要一个书架，她请Larry 陪她去社区看看有没有卖旧货的。李华今天会学到两个常用语：up for something和spot someone。</p>
<p>LH: Larry ， 我想买个书架， 你陪我去附近社区看看有没有卖旧货的，好吗？对了，报纸上的广告说， 附近就有一家， 走路10分钟就到。</p>
<p>LL: Sure. I&#8217;m up for it. Let&#8217;s go.</p>
<p>LH: Up for it? 这话是什么意思？听起来好像你愿意去。</p>
<p>LL: Right! When someone says he is up for it, that just means they are ready to do what is proposed, often some kind of social activity.</p>
<p>LH: 明白了， 你刚才说，“I&#8217;m up for it”, 意思是你愿意跟我去社区看看卖旧货的情况。那太好了。Larry ， 如果我买到书架， 你能不能帮我把它搬回去呀？</p>
<p>LL: I&#8217;m definitely not up for that. I definitely don&#8217;t want to do that.</p>
<p>LH: 我要是请你吃晚饭呢？Are you up for it ?</p>
<p>LL: Well, I could be up for that, as long as the furniture isn&#8217;t too big or heavy.</p>
<p>LH: 瞧你，请你吃饭就原意帮我搬。当然咯，要是书桌太大，太重，你一个人是搬不动的。可是我也不要太大的。</p>
<p>LL： Li Hua, I&#8217;d like to get some exercise today, would you be up for going for a run later today?</p>
<p>LH：跟你去跑步？ 好啊。 I&#8217;m up for it! 我最近也一直没有运动，体重增加了两磅，正需要锻练呢！</p>
<p>LL: I meant to ask you, Li Hua, we spend so much time together, do you think your parents are up for you hanging out with me instead of your Chinese friends?</p>
<p>LH: 我父母没有意见， 因为跟你交往， 我既可以提高英语又能更多地了解美国生活。这是件好事呀。 我倒要问问你， 你的父母愿意我们两个作朋友吗？</p>
<p>LL: Of course they are up for it. They like you and you help me improve my Chinese.</p>
<p>LH：我也很喜欢你爸爸妈妈呀！至于帮你学中文嘛，我看成效不大。你看，你从来也不跟我讲中文。</p>
<p>LL：Well, when I have a bigger vocabulary of Chinese characters, I will certainly feel up for speaking Chinese with you.</p>
<p>LH：你总是说等我增加词汇量后再讲。我看，你永远也开不了口！</p>
<p>******</p>
<p>(嘈杂人声)</p>
<p>LH: Larry，你看那家的门口摆了不少东西在卖， 我们去看看。</p>
<p>LL: Yeah, I might even want to get something too, though I didn&#8217;t bring my wallet. If I see something I like, do you think you could spot me?</p>
<p>LH: 你可能要买东西，但没带钱包。你要做什么？Spot you？ 找你呀！</p>
<p>LL: No, that&#8217;s not what I meant. If someone asks you if you can spot him or her, it means that he wants to know if you can lend him some money.</p>
<p>LH: 噢，原来spot someone在这里是指借钱给某人。 你要是看到喜欢的东西，我当然可以借钱给你呀！可不能太贵了，否则我买书架钱就不够了。</p>
<p>LL: Hey, look at that bookcase, that would be perfect for your dormitory.</p>
<p>LH: 记得我刚来纽约的时候，在商店里看见过一个特别漂亮的书架，可是价格太贵， 我买不起，当时也不认识什么能够借钱给我的人。</p>
<p>LL: Oh, if only I had known you then, I would have spotted you. But you can buy the bookcase here now. It&#8217;s a real nice one.</p>
<p>LH: 这个书架是挺好，价钱也不贵。 我实在是需要，否则我也不买，因为，你知道，我毕业后回国的时候， 这些东西不是送掉，就是扔掉。</p>
<p>LL: That&#8217;s right. And you need a lot money to buy airplane ticket and gifts for your family.</p>
<p>LH: 到时候我能向银行借钱吗？Can I ask a bank to spot me?</p>
<p>LL: No. Spot someone usually involves a small amount of money, while bank loans are often a big sum.</p>
<p>LH：噢，银行贷款一般数额大一些，那spot someone都是借少量的钱。</p>
<p>LL：Right. Li Hua, can you spot me for that badminton racquet over there?</p>
<p>LH: 你要买那羽毛球拍呀？ 才三美元呐，我可以借钱给你，没问题。不过你必须帮我把书架抬到宿舍去。</p>
<p>LL: Sure, I&#8217;ll help you since you&#8217;re so nice to spot me for that badminton racquet.</p>
<p>今天李华学到了两个常用语，一个是up for something。意思是愿意作某件事。李华学到的另一个常用语是spot someone。意思是借钱给某个人。<font color=#ffffff>(http://www.dajiyuan.com)</font></p>
