<tr><td VALIGN=TOP width="626"><h2 align=center>流行美语 第206课</h2>
<h6></h6>
<hr>
<p>【大纪元12月15日讯】<center><ahref=http://www.freexinwen.com/chinese/eng/pa/download.asp?file=/chinese/s_pa/pa206.mp3>(MP3下载)</a></center><br />这是个周末，Larry和李华在Larry叔叔的农场里摘了一天的苹果。李华会学到两个常用语：chock full和doohickey.</p>
<p>LL: Wow, Li Hua, your basket is chock full of apples. </p>
<p>LH: Chock full of apples？你是不是说我的篮子都装满了？ </p>
<p>LL: Yeah, chock full means full to the point of overflowing, and I don&#8217;t think you could put another apple in that basket. </p>
<p>LH: 噢，chock full是指篮子不仅满了，而且是满得快掉出来了。那也是，我这篮子里是一个也放不下了。Larry，你的篮子怎么还没有装满呀？ </p>
<p>LL: You are right. My basket is not as chock full of apples as yours, but I have an excuse! </p>
<p>LH: Excuse！什么理由？ </p>
<p>LL: When I&#8217;m out picking apples on a nice, sunny day in the fresh air, I start to think about things. Soon my mind is chock full of ideas, but I&#8217;m afraid my basket isn&#8217;t chock full of apples. </p>
<p>LH: 我才不信呢！什么一到阳光明媚，空气新鲜的环境里摘苹果，你就开始动脑子。那你倒说说，今天都想了些什么呀！ </p>
<p>LL: I was thinking of things we could do with these apples. My aunt has a cabinet in the kitchen chock full of recipe books. </p>
<p>LH: 你在想怎么处理这些苹果? 得了，这是你动作慢的借口罢了。不过，我对你说的你阿姨厨房里的食谱倒很感兴趣，咱们去找她聊聊吧。 </p>
<p>LL: And while we are in the kitchen talking to my aunt, we can have a little snack. She has a jar on the counter that is chock full of cookies. </p>
<p>LH: 饼干我就不吃了， 太油太甜，They are chock full of sugar and fat. </p>
<p>LL: Oh, a little sugar and fat won&#8217;t hurt you, Li Hua. Besides the cookies are chock full of things that are good for you, like nuts, oatmeal and peanut butter. </p>
<p>LH: 饼干里有果仁，麦片和花生酱？这些都是对身体有好处的？多吃了也是一样不好，你只是嘴巴谗而已！ </p>
<p>LL: Ha, ha, Li Hua. Very funny. Hey, while we on are way to the kitchen, let&#8217;s stop by my uncle&#8217;s tool shed. </p>
<p>LH: 你要先去看看你叔叔的工具房？ 好呀！ </p>
<p>****** </p>
<p>LL：Boy, I forgot my uncle has so many tools. Can you hand me that doohickey on the tool bench? </p>
<p>LH: Doohickey是什么呀？ </p>
<p>LL: Oh, I should have been more specific, Li Hua. A doohickey is a generic term for a tool or a gadget. </p>
<p>LH: Doohickey是工具或小型工具的总称。那你要的是哪个呀？ </p>
<p>LL: I want the doohickey in the middle of the bench. </p>
<p>LH: 在板凳当中的那个， 给你。Larry，这个东西是干什么用的？ </p>
<p>LL: This doohickey is one of the greatest inventions of all time. It is an apple corer, peeler and slicer. </p>
<p>LH: 这玩艺儿既能削皮，挖掉苹果的核还能把苹果切成片，有这么多功能呀。怪不得你说它是最伟大的发明之一。 </p>
<p>LL: Yes, it does. This doohickey may look simple, but it is a good tool to have in a country kitchen when you have a lot of apples. </p>
<p>LH: 当然啦，在这样的农村厨房里，摘了那么苹果，有这么一个工具当然好呀！这个工具怎么用的？ </p>
<p>LL: Well, first you stick the entire doohickey on a flat surface of a table. </p>
<p>LH: OK， 先把这工具放在平的桌面上。我来试试， 看看这个东西是怎么切苹果的。 </p>
<p>LL: Put the apple here like this. And then turn the handle of the doohickey. And there you have it &#8212; a cored, peeled, and slice apple! </p>
<p>LH: 把苹果放在这里，然后转动这个把手。真的耶，你看，皮也削了，核也没了，而且已经切成片了。 </p>
<p>LL: Isn&#8217;t this doohickey great? </p>
<p>LH: Wow! 真有意思， 你叔叔的工具房里还有什么好的小玩艺儿呀? Any other interesting doohickeys? </p>
<p>今天李华学到了两个常用语。一个是chock full , 意思是装的满满的。还有一个是doohickeys，意思是工具。<font color=#ffffff>(http://www.dajiyuan.com)</font></p>
