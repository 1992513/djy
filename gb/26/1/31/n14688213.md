<img width="600" src="https://i.epochtimes.com/assets/uploads/2026/01/id14688223-IMG_6281-600x400.jpg" />
<h6>2026年1月31日傍晚，秦晋博士受邀在悉尼市政厅举行的伊朗社区集会上用英文发表演讲。（夏楚君/大纪元）</h6>
<hr>
<p>【大纪元2026年01月31日讯】【编者按：1月31日傍晚，秦晋博士受邀在悉尼市政厅举行的伊朗社区集会上发表演讲。他回顾了伊朗自1970年代以来的历史转折，直指1979年宗教革命后政教合一体制给伊朗社会带来的长期苦难。他认为，近半个世纪的民众抗争在高压统治下付出惨痛代价，仅靠内部反抗已难以改变现状；唯有“霹雳手段的正义”，即国际力量的实质性介入，才能终结毛拉政权，为伊朗人民带来解放与重建的契机。下面是演讲的中英文全文】</p>
<h2>秦晋博士：霹雳手段的正义才能解救伊朗</h2>
<p>很荣幸受到今天集会的组织者的邀请并做一个发言。我是秦晋，中国民主与自由的追求者。</p>
<p>我最早知道伊朗，是在 1974 年亚洲运动会。伊朗以东道国身份出现在亚洲舞台中央，距今已大约半个世纪。彼时的伊朗，尚处在 巴列维国王（穆罕默德·礼萨·巴列维）统治之下，是一个在冷战格局中明显向西方倾斜、以现代化与世俗化为发展方向的国家。</p>
<p>伊朗的变天是1979年的霍梅尼宗教革命，那场革命得到了伊朗民众的支持，也得到了国际社会的默许。从此伊朗成为了一个政教合一的国度。</p>
<p>革命的结果，是伊朗从一个亲西方的世俗君主制国家，转变为一个以什叶派神权为核心的政教合一体制。霍梅尼提出的“法基赫监护”（Velayat-e Faqih）原则，确立了宗教领袖在国家政治中的最高权威。这一制度设计，既是对伊朗传统宗教结构的制度化，也是对现代民族国家形式的一种独特重构。</p>
<p>从此，伊朗走上了一条与1970年代想像中截然不同的发展道路：在意识形态上与西方全面对立，在政治结构上强调宗教合法性，在对外关系上不断与国际体系发生摩擦。</p>
<p>伊朗人民经历了巨大的苦难，近半个世纪来，发生过多次的民众抗争，但是都被无情地镇压了下去。</p>
<p>而最近爆发的全国性抗议，据当地卫生官员称，伊朗抗议活动死亡人数可能超过3万人。仅仅依靠伊朗人民前赴后继的抗争，是无法战胜哈梅内伊毛拉政权的。原因很简单，这个政权极其冷血，无情杀戮任何敢于反抗的普通民众，血肉之躯在无情杀戮面前极其脆弱，不堪一击。</p>
<p>BBC认为专制政权的消亡是一个渐进又突发的过程，但伊朗还未到那一步。我认为BBC的观点大错特错，伊朗已经到了毛拉政权消亡的地步。原因是国际力量的介入，也就是美国的介入。联合国为首的国际社会对面对伊朗人民的苦难无动于衷，他们能够提供的只是口惠而实不至的虚伪，甚至与世界上所有邪恶势力沆瀣一气。</p>
<p>现在情况发生了变化，美国出现了一位不讲政治正确的总统，我相信他会出手消灭伊朗毛拉政权，解放伊朗。我也很有耐心但很急迫地期待他兑现对伊朗人民的承诺Help is on the way。至于伊朗在毛拉政权被击溃以后走向何方，则是伊朗人民的自己选择。</p>
<p>回望1974年亚运会那个仍然自信、开放、意图融入世界的伊朗，再对照革命之后的伊朗轨迹，历史的断裂与反转，尤显意味深长。</p>
<p>我站在这里，表达我对伊朗人民摆脱伊朗毛拉政权的残酷统治这一诉求的理解和支持，同时希望伊朗人民不要再犯47年前的错误。期盼伊朗人民获得解放，重建伊朗，让伊朗为世界走向和平、稳定、富庶做出贡献，让伊朗成为中东一盏闪耀的明灯。</p>
<figure id="attachment_14688224" aria-describedby="caption-attachment-14688224" style="width: 600px" class="wp-caption aligncenter"><a target="_blank" href="https://i.epochtimes.com/assets/uploads/2026/01/id14688224-IMG_6287.jpg"><img loading="lazy" decoding="async" class="size-large wp-image-14688224" src="https://i.epochtimes.com/assets/uploads/2026/01/id14688224-IMG_6287-600x400.jpg" alt="" width="600" b="400" /></a><figcaption id="caption-attachment-14688224" class="wp-caption-text">2026年1月31日傍晚，秦晋博士在悉尼市政厅举行的伊朗社区集会上，与声援伊朗革命的华人合影。（夏楚君/大纪元）</figcaption></figure>
<h3><span class="ng-star-inserted">Dr. Chin Jin: Forceful Justice Is the Only Way to liberate? Iran</span></h3>
<p>It is a great honor to have been invited to address the rally.? I am Chin Jin, a pursuer of democracy and freedom in China.</p>
<p>The earliest I learned about Iran was during the 1974 Asian Games. As the host state, Iran stood at the center of the Asian stage, and that was nearly half a century ago. At that time, Iran was still under the rule of King Pahlavi, a nation clearly aligned with the West in the Cold War pattern, pursuing modernization and secularization as its developmental direction.</p>
<p>Iran&#8217;s dramatic change came with the 1979 Khomeini Islamic Revolution, a revolution that received support from the Iranian people and tacit approval from the international community. From then on, Iran became a theocratic state where religion and politics were fused.</p>
<p>The outcome of the revolution was Iran&#8217;s transformation from a pro-Western secular monarchy into a theocratic system centered on Shia clerical rule. The principle proposed by Khomeini established the supreme authority of the religious leader in national politics. This institutional design both formalized Iran&#8217;s traditional religious structure and represented a unique reconfiguration of the modern nation-state form.</p>
<p>From that point, Iran embarked on a development path radically different from what was imagined in the 1970s: ideologically in complete opposition to the West, politically emphasizing religious legitimacy, and in foreign relations constantly clashing with the international system.</p>
<p>The Iranian have endured immense suffering. Over nearly half a century, there have been multiple waves of popular resistance, but all were ruthlessly suppressed.</p>
<p>In the most recent nationwide protests, according to reliable sources, the death toll may exceed 30,000. Relying solely on the Iranian successive acts of resistance is insufficient to defeat the mullah regime under Khamenei. The reason is simple: this regime is extremely ruthless, mercilessly slaughtering any ordinary citizens who dare to resist. Human flesh and blood are extremely fragile and vulnerable in the face of such relentless killing.</p>
<p>The BBC believes that the demise of authoritarian regimes is a process that is both gradual and sudden, but Iran has not yet reached that point. I think the BBC&#8217;s view is profoundly mistaken—Iran has already reached the stage where the mullah regime is on the verge of collapse. The reason lies in the intervention of international forces, specifically that of the United States.</p>
<p>The international community, led by the United Nations, has remained indifferent to the suffering of the Iranian people; what they can offer is nothing more than empty words and hypocritical gestures, or even colluding with all the evil forces in the world. Now the situation has changed: the United States has a president who does not adhere to political correctness, and I believe he will take action to eliminate the Iranian mullah regime and liberate Iran. I am both patient and urgently awaiting him to fulfill his promise to the Iranian: &#8220;Help is on the way.&#8221;</p>
<p>As for the direction Iran will take after the mullah regime is defeated, that will be the choice of the Iranian themselves.</p>
<figure id="attachment_14688226" aria-describedby="caption-attachment-14688226" style="width: 600px" class="wp-caption aligncenter"><a target="_blank" href="https://i.epochtimes.com/assets/uploads/2026/01/id14688226-IMG_6236.jpg"><img loading="lazy" decoding="async" class="size-large wp-image-14688226" src="https://i.epochtimes.com/assets/uploads/2026/01/id14688226-IMG_6236-600x400.jpg" alt="" width="600" b="400" /></a><figcaption id="caption-attachment-14688226" class="wp-caption-text">2026年1月31日傍晚，悉尼伊朗社区再度在市政厅举行集会。集会人士手举这是“最后的一战”标语牌。（夏楚君/大纪元）</figcaption></figure>
<p>Looking back at the confident, open Iran of the 1974 Asian Games—one that sought to integrate into the world—and comparing it to the trajectory of Iran after the 1979 revolution, the rupture and reversal of history feel especially profound and meaningful.</p>
<p>Standing here, I express my understanding and support for the Iranian demand to break free from the cruel rule of the Iranian mullah regime, while hoping that the Iranian will not repeat the mistakes made 47 years ago. I look forward to the liberation of the Iranian, to the rebuilding of Iran, so that Iran can contribute to a world moving toward peace, stability, and prosperity, and become a shining beacon in the Middle East.</p>
