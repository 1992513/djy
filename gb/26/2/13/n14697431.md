<tr><td VALIGN=TOP width="626"><h2 align=center>【看电影学英文】‘Mina’(3) A Piece of Me</h2>
<img width="600" src="https://i.epochtimes.com/assets/uploads/2026/02/id14697450-1280-600x400.png" />
<h6>《美娜传奇》动画片视频截图（干净世界提供，甄宇合成）</h6>
<hr>
<p>前两期聊了动画片《<ahref="https://www.ganjingworld.com/s/GYA6RxQVQw" rel="nofollow"><u>美娜传奇</u></a>》中跟奇幻世界相关的一些英文，以及跟美娜和她的追求者们相关的英文，这次我们侧重看一些竞争和冲突时常见的单词与习语。</p>
<h2>小试牛刀：</h2>
<p>下面是动画片《<ahref="https://www.ganjingworld.com/s/GYA6RxQVQw" rel="nofollow"><u>美娜传奇</u></a>》中几句对白，若是你，会怎么翻译？</p>
<div>
<div id="ggWrapper_z_a_c_1" class="ggAdZone_gg-banner-ad_container__nctW7 ggAdZone_gg-banner-ad_in_article_container__OT2Hh" data-ggwrapper-uniqid="z_a_c_1|1770964402087" data-ggwrapper-zoneid="z_a_c_1" data-ggwrapper-extzoneid="z_a_c_1">1）You are so done.<br />
问题：是“你是这样完成了”的意思吗？2）I’ll buy you some time.<br />
问题：是“我会给你买些时间”的意思吗？3）You want a piece of me?！<br />
问题：是“你想要一片我”的意思吗？4）I&#8217;m in the zone.<br />
问题：是“我在区内”的意思吗？如果你对上面几句的翻译不太有把握，那下面的内容可能会对你有所帮助。</p>
<h2><strong>You’ll all get it</strong></h2>
<p>“Get it”有明白的意思，但在负面语境中，“You&#8217;ll all get it”有威胁口吻，意思是你们会得到应有惩处的。</p>
<p>Mina: Stop! I’ll tell my dad and you’ll all get it.<br />
美娜：住手！我会告诉父王，你们会被惩处的。</p>
<figure id="attachment_14697451" aria-describedby="caption-attachment-14697451" style="width: 600px" class="wp-caption aligncenter"><a target="_blank" href="https://i.epochtimes.com/assets/uploads/2026/02/id14697451-1280_1.webp"><img decoding="async" class=" wp-image-14697451" src="https://i.epochtimes.com/assets/uploads/2026/02/id14697451-1280_1-450x253.webp" alt="" width="600" b="337" /></a><figcaption id="caption-attachment-14697451" class="wp-caption-text">美娜威胁饶阁岱，为罗南解围。（干净世界视频截图）</figcaption></figure>
<h2><strong>You are so done</strong></h2>
<p>Done 虽然有完成的意思，但“You are so done”跟完成无关，而是表示某人彻底厌倦、极度沮丧，已经到达忍耐的极限，再也无法忍受下去，可翻译为“你彻底完蛋了”“你死定了”或“你没戏了”。</p>
<p>Rogdai: You are so done.<br />
饶阁岱：你死定了。</p>
<figure id="attachment_14697452" aria-describedby="caption-attachment-14697452" style="width: 600px" class="wp-caption aligncenter"><a target="_blank" href="https://i.epochtimes.com/assets/uploads/2026/02/id14697452-1280_2.webp"><img decoding="async" class=" wp-image-14697452" src="https://i.epochtimes.com/assets/uploads/2026/02/id14697452-1280_2.webp" alt="" width="600" b="337" /></a><figcaption id="caption-attachment-14697452" class="wp-caption-text">饶阁岱想置罗南于死地。（干净世界视频截图）</figcaption></figure>
<p>“You are so done”更常见的变体是“I&#8217;m so done”，可翻译为“我真受够了”。</p>
<p>I’m so done with this weather.<br />
我真的受够这鬼天气了！</p>
<p>Lots more to do with my report. I’m just so done with writing it.<br />
论文还有很多要写的。我真是写够了！</p>
<h2><strong>Buy you some time</strong></h2>
<p>“Buy you some time”可不是买时间，时间也买不到。这里的 buy 是获取、争取的意思，buy time 就是拖延时间、争取时间的意思。动画片《<ahref="https://www.ganjingworld.com/s/GYA6RxQVQw" rel="nofollow"><u>美娜传奇</u></a>》中，危险来临，美娜不是着急自己赶快跑，而是无私殿后，为其他公主争取时间。</p>
<p>Mina: The dragon is coming. I’ll buy you some time.<br />
美娜：那龙来了。我给你们争取些时间。</p>
<figure id="attachment_14697453" aria-describedby="caption-attachment-14697453" style="width: 600px" class="wp-caption aligncenter"><a target="_blank" href="https://i.epochtimes.com/assets/uploads/2026/02/id14697453-1280_3.webp"><img loading="lazy" decoding="async" class=" wp-image-14697453" src="https://i.epochtimes.com/assets/uploads/2026/02/id14697453-1280_3.webp" alt="" width="600" b="337" /></a><figcaption id="caption-attachment-14697453" class="wp-caption-text">美娜无私殿后为他人争取时间。（干净世界视频截图）</figcaption></figure>
<h2><strong>A piece of me</strong></h2>
<p>在问句中，“a piece of me”不是说“一片我”，而是说“想打架吗？”既可以用在准备真正动手的场景，也可以用在游戏或竞技中开玩笑式的挑衅。类似于 “You wanna go?”</p>
<p>Mina: You want a piece of me?！<br />
美娜：你想打架吗？</p>
<figure id="attachment_14697454" aria-describedby="caption-attachment-14697454" style="width: 600px" class="wp-caption aligncenter"><a target="_blank" href="https://i.epochtimes.com/assets/uploads/2026/02/id14697454-1280_4.webp"><img loading="lazy" decoding="async" class=" wp-image-14697454" src="https://i.epochtimes.com/assets/uploads/2026/02/id14697454-1280_4.webp" alt="" width="600" b="337" /></a><figcaption id="caption-attachment-14697454" class="wp-caption-text">面对恶龙，美娜一点都不怯阵。（干净世界视频截图）</figcaption></figure>
<h2><strong>Bring it on</strong></h2>
<p>意思是“放马过来”“出招吧”“来吧”，是充满自信的表达方式，常用于竞争中，表明已准备好应对任何挑战或挑衅。</p>
<p>If they want an early election, that&#8217;s fine by us. Bring it on!<br />
如果他们想提前选举，我们无所谓。来吧！</p>
<p>类似的说法还有“Bring it”, “Bring them on”, 以及 “Bring your stuff”（亮出你的家伙吧）。</p>
<figure id="attachment_14697455" aria-describedby="caption-attachment-14697455" style="width: 600px" class="wp-caption aligncenter"><a target="_blank" href="https://i.epochtimes.com/assets/uploads/2026/02/id14697455-1280_5.webp"><img loading="lazy" decoding="async" class=" wp-image-14697455" src="https://i.epochtimes.com/assets/uploads/2026/02/id14697455-1280_5.webp" alt="" width="600" b="337" /></a><figcaption id="caption-attachment-14697455" class="wp-caption-text">美娜挑战恶龙。（干净世界视频截图）</figcaption></figure>
<h2><strong>Serve you right</strong></h2>
<p>意为活该，带有认为对方罪有应得、自作自受的幸灾乐祸的语气。</p>
<p>Mina: Take that! Serves you right!<br />
美娜：看招！活该！</p>
<p>这里的“Take that”是打斗时喊的话，相当于“接招”“吃我一拳”之类的。</p>
<p>You lowlifes! Take that! And that! And that!<br />
你们这些下三滥！看招！再来！还有这招！</p>
<figure id="attachment_14697456" aria-describedby="caption-attachment-14697456" style="width: 600px" class="wp-caption aligncenter"><a target="_blank" href="https://i.epochtimes.com/assets/uploads/2026/02/id14697456-1280_6.webp"><img loading="lazy" decoding="async" class=" wp-image-14697456" src="https://i.epochtimes.com/assets/uploads/2026/02/id14697456-1280_6.webp" alt="" width="600" b="337" /></a><figcaption id="caption-attachment-14697456" class="wp-caption-text">美娜痛殴卡匝伯尔。（干净世界视频截图）</figcaption></figure>
<h2><strong>Draft pick</strong></h2>
<p>Draft 除了草案、汇票、兵役制、冷空气、散装（啤酒）等意思，还可以指美国职业联盟（如NBA、NFL）各球队在赛季之初选拔新队员的选秀制度。Draft pick 指在年度选秀大会上挑选新球员的权利，或具体指代被选中的球员。</p>
<p>First-round draft pick 首轮选秀球员或首轮选秀权</p>
<p>Top draft pick 顶级选秀球员</p>
<p>Rogdai: I paid a visit to the King this afternoon. By the look on his face, I&#8217;d say I&#8217;m his number one draft pick to marry Mina.<br />
饶阁岱：下午我去拜见国王。看他表情，我应该是他的驸马首选。</p>
<figure id="attachment_14697457" aria-describedby="caption-attachment-14697457" style="width: 600px" class="wp-caption aligncenter"><a target="_blank" href="https://i.epochtimes.com/assets/uploads/2026/02/id14697457-1280_7.webp"><img loading="lazy" decoding="async" class=" wp-image-14697457" src="https://i.epochtimes.com/assets/uploads/2026/02/id14697457-1280_7.webp" alt="" width="600" b="337" /></a><figcaption id="caption-attachment-14697457" class="wp-caption-text">饶格岱觉得自己是驸马首选。（干净世界视频截图）</figcaption></figure>
<h2><strong>In the zone</strong></h2>
<p>指人处于巅峰状态，做事得心应手。</p>
<p>Kabir: You&#8217;re totally in the zone, Rogdai.<br />
凯博：饶阁岱，你今天很在状态啊。</p>
<figure id="attachment_14697458" aria-describedby="caption-attachment-14697458" style="width: 600px" class="wp-caption aligncenter"><a target="_blank" href="https://i.epochtimes.com/assets/uploads/2026/02/id14697458-1280_8.webp"><img loading="lazy" decoding="async" class=" wp-image-14697458" src="https://i.epochtimes.com/assets/uploads/2026/02/id14697458-1280_8.webp" alt="" width="600" b="337" /></a><figcaption id="caption-attachment-14697458" class="wp-caption-text">凯博觉得饶格岱今天很在状态。（干净世界视频截图）</figcaption></figure>
<h2><strong>On a winning streak</strong></h2>
<p>Streak 用作名词的时候，除了条纹、特征等意思，还有一连串、一阵子、一系列的意思。在体育比赛、博彩或业务中，短时间内连续取得成功或胜利就是?on a winning streak，而屡战屡败是 on a losing streak，好运连连则是 lucky streak。</p>
<p>Rogdai: I&#8217;m on a real winning streak, Kabir.<br />
饶阁岱：凯博，我真的是得心应手。</p>
<figure id="attachment_14697459" aria-describedby="caption-attachment-14697459" style="width: 600px" class="wp-caption aligncenter"><a target="_blank" href="https://i.epochtimes.com/assets/uploads/2026/02/id14697459-1280_9.webp"><img loading="lazy" decoding="async" class=" wp-image-14697459" src="https://i.epochtimes.com/assets/uploads/2026/02/id14697459-1280_9.webp" alt="" width="600" b="337" /></a><figcaption id="caption-attachment-14697459" class="wp-caption-text">饶阁岱自己也觉得很得心应手。（干净世界视频截图）</figcaption></figure>
<h2><strong>Hooray</strong></h2>
<p>Hooray 是英语中常用的感叹词，是人们在感到兴奋或庆祝成功时发出的欢呼或叫喊声，可翻译为“万岁”“好哇”“太棒了”。跟 hurray 和 hurrah 的意思基本相同。</p>
<p>Hooray! We won!<br />
万岁！我们赢了！</p>
<p>Hip, hip, hooray!<br />
嗨，嗨，万岁！（常见庆祝口号，通常是一人喊“Hip,hip”，其他人回应“Hooray”）</p>
<p>Hooray! It&#8217;s finally Friday!<br />
好哇！终于周五了！</p>
<p>King: Let’s start the celebration!<br />
国王：让我们开始庆祝吧！</p>
<p>Mina: Hooray! That’s so wonderful!<br />
美娜：好耶！太棒啦！</p>
<figure id="attachment_14697460" aria-describedby="caption-attachment-14697460" style="width: 600px" class="wp-caption aligncenter"><a target="_blank" href="https://i.epochtimes.com/assets/uploads/2026/02/id14697460-1280_10.webp"><img loading="lazy" decoding="async" class=" wp-image-14697460" src="https://i.epochtimes.com/assets/uploads/2026/02/id14697460-1280_10.webp" alt="" width="600" b="337" /></a><figcaption id="caption-attachment-14697460" class="wp-caption-text">真相大白，众人欢庆。（干净世界视频截图）</figcaption></figure>
