<p>下面是影片<ahref="https://www.ganjingworld.com/s/GYA6RxQVQw" rel="nofollow"><u>《</u><u>美娜传奇</u><u>》</u></a>中几句对白。若是你，会怎么翻译？</p>

<div id="ggWrapper_z_a_c_1" class="ggAdZone_gg-banner-ad_container__nctW7 ggAdZone_gg-banner-ad_in_article_container__OT2Hh" data-ggwrapper-uniqid="z_a_c_1|1771552927306" data-ggwrapper-zoneid="z_a_c_1" data-ggwrapper-extzoneid="z_a_c_1">1）I&#8217;m in your debt.</div>

<p>问题：是“我欠你钱”的意思吗？</p>
<p>2）Sit around<br />
问题：是“坐成一圈”的意思吗？</p>
<p>3）Don&#8217;t fail me.<br />
问题：是“别让我失败”的意思吗？</p>
<p>4）A scaredy-cat<br />
问题：是“一只吓人的猫”吗？</p>
<p>如果你对上面几句的翻译不太有把握，那下面的内容可能会对你有所帮助。</p>
<h2><strong>In someone&#8217;s debt</strong></h2>
<p>意思是别人帮了大忙，自己非常感激，觉得欠对方人情，是一种非常礼貌且带有文学色彩的表达，常用于正式场合或表达深深的谢意。类似的表达还有“I owe you one”。</p>
<p>I do not want to be in your debt, so I will solve this problem myself.<br />
不想欠你人情，所以我会自己解决这问题的。</p>
<p>Bjorn: Thank you, brave warrior. You saved me and my family. I&#8217;m in your debt and at your service.<br />
熊爸爸：感谢您，勇敢的斗士。您救了我和我的家人。我欠您情，愿随时为您效劳。</p>
<figure id="attachment_14701958" aria-describedby="caption-attachment-14701958" style="width: 1280px" class="wp-caption aligncenter"><a target="_blank" href="https://i.epochtimes.com/assets/uploads/2026/02/id14701958-1280_1.webp"><img decoding="async" class="size-full wp-image-14701958" src="https://i.epochtimes.com/assets/uploads/2026/02/id14701958-1280_1.webp" alt="" width="1280" b="720" /></a><figcaption id="caption-attachment-14701958" class="wp-caption-text">熊爸爸感念罗南恩情，想帮他。（干净世界视频截图）</figcaption></figure>
<p>“欠人情”是“in someone&#8217;s debt”，那“还人情”该怎么说呢？可以说“return the favor”或“repay someone for something”，这两种说法都是报答某人，还人情的意思。</p>
<h2><strong>Don&#8217;t fail me</strong></h2>
<p>常用于表达对某人的信任与期盼，希望对方能把事情做好，不辜负期望，可以翻译为“别让我失望”“别掉链子”，与“Don&#8217;t let me down”意思相近。</p>
<p>I feel I’ve failed my children by not spending more time with them.<br />
我感觉自己对不起孩子们，因为我没有花更多时间陪伴他们。</p>
<p>Kezabor: Don’t fail me or I will make you suffer in the ways you’ve never heard of!<br />
卡匝伯尔：被让我失望，否则我会用你们连聼都没听过的方式折磨你们！</p>
<figure id="attachment_14701959" aria-describedby="caption-attachment-14701959" style="width: 1280px" class="wp-caption aligncenter"><a target="_blank" href="https://i.epochtimes.com/assets/uploads/2026/02/id14701959-1280_2.webp"><img decoding="async" class="size-full wp-image-14701959" src="https://i.epochtimes.com/assets/uploads/2026/02/id14701959-1280_2.webp" alt="" width="1280" b="720" /></a><figcaption id="caption-attachment-14701959" class="wp-caption-text">卡匝伯尔逼迫手下寻找美娜。（干净世界视频截图）</figcaption></figure>
<h2><strong>Shed your doubts</strong></h2>
<p>Shed 做动词时，意思是去除、摆脱、蜕皮等。“Shed your doubts”是一个鼓励人坚定信心、克服不安的短语，意为“抛弃你的疑虑”“消除你的怀疑”或“别再犹豫”。</p>
<p>Nahina: Shed your doubts, nothing to fear!<br />
娜希娜：不要犹豫，没什么可怕的！</p>
<figure id="attachment_14701960" aria-describedby="caption-attachment-14701960" style="width: 450px" class="wp-caption aligncenter"><a target="_blank" href="https://i.epochtimes.com/assets/uploads/2026/02/id14701960-1280_3.webp"><img loading="lazy" decoding="async" src="https://i.epochtimes.com/assets/uploads/2026/02/id14701960-1280_3-450x253.webp" alt="" width="450" b="253" class="size-medium wp-image-14701960" /></a><figcaption id="caption-attachment-14701960" class="wp-caption-text">娜希娜蛊惑美娜接受卡匝伯尔。（干净世界视频截图）</figcaption></figure>
<h2><strong>Deal</strong></h2>
<p>Deal 的主要意思是交易、协议、处理、应付等，在口语中表示优惠，还有成交、一言为定的意思。</p>
<p>Rogdai: Deal! Let’s see how your brain does against my axe.<br />
饶阁岱：一言为定！让我们看看你的脑子能不能扛得住我这把斧头！</p>
<figure id="attachment_14701961" aria-describedby="caption-attachment-14701961" style="width: 1280px" class="wp-caption aligncenter"><a target="_blank" href="https://i.epochtimes.com/assets/uploads/2026/02/id14701961-1280_4.webp"><img loading="lazy" decoding="async" class="size-full wp-image-14701961" src="https://i.epochtimes.com/assets/uploads/2026/02/id14701961-1280_4.webp" alt="" width="1280" b="720" /></a><figcaption id="caption-attachment-14701961" class="wp-caption-text">饶阁岱挑战罗南。（干净世界视频截图）</figcaption></figure>
<p>下面是一些跟 deal 有关的习惯用语：</p>
<p>Big deal 重要的事</p>
<p>Make a big deal 是指大惊小怪、言过其实、过度重视等意思</p>
<p>No big deal 没什么大不了的</p>
<p>What’s the deal? 是老外常用的问句，意思是“情况如何？”相当于“What’s the problem?”或“What&#8217;s the situation?”</p>
<p>Here&#8217;s the deal. 是在谈判时候常用的发语词，相当于“这样好了”，接下来会说出建议具体内容。</p>
<p>Deal me in. 源自纸牌游戏发牌时要包含某人，意思是“加我进去”或“把我也算在内”，类似于“Count me in”。“把我算在这次之外”则是“Deal me out”。</p>
<h2><strong>No biggie</strong></h2>
<p>Biggie 是一个英语俚语名词，意思是大人物、要人、大亨、大事情或重要的事。No biggie 就是小事一桩、没什么大不了的、没关系。</p>
<p>Pha Chan: A giant? No biggie! We know how to deal with a giant. Isn’t that right, Donut?<br />
陈发：巨人？小事一桩！我们知道怎样对付一个巨人。对吧，甜甜圈（他骑的驴名叫Donut）？</p>
<figure id="attachment_14701962" aria-describedby="caption-attachment-14701962" style="width: 1280px" class="wp-caption aligncenter"><a target="_blank" href="https://i.epochtimes.com/assets/uploads/2026/02/id14701962-1280_5.webp"><img loading="lazy" decoding="async" class="size-full wp-image-14701962" src="https://i.epochtimes.com/assets/uploads/2026/02/id14701962-1280_5.webp" alt="" width="1280" b="720" /></a><figcaption id="caption-attachment-14701962" class="wp-caption-text">陈发想起妈妈说如何对付巨人。（干净世界视频截图）</figcaption></figure>
<h2><strong>Come in handy?</strong></h2>
<p>常用于日常英语中，用来表达物品在需要时非常方便，可以派上用场。</p>
<p>Mina: This boat will really come in handy.<br />
美娜：这艘船会派上用场。</p>
<figure id="attachment_14701963" aria-describedby="caption-attachment-14701963" style="width: 1280px" class="wp-caption aligncenter"><a target="_blank" href="https://i.epochtimes.com/assets/uploads/2026/02/id14701963-1280_6.webp"><img loading="lazy" decoding="async" class="size-full wp-image-14701963" src="https://i.epochtimes.com/assets/uploads/2026/02/id14701963-1280_6.webp" alt="" width="1280" b="720" /></a><figcaption id="caption-attachment-14701963" class="wp-caption-text">若不能嫁给罗南，美娜打算跟他乘船逃走。（干净世界视频截图）</figcaption></figure>
<h2><strong>Lecture me to death</strong></h2>
<p>这是一个夸张的习语，用来表示某人给你进行了一场没完没了的、烦人的训话或说教，让你感到极度疲惫，难以忍受，可翻译为“把我唠叨死了”或“把我教训死了”。</p>
<p>My mom just lectured me to death about being late.<br />
晚归的事，我妈刚才把我唠叨死了。</p>
<figure id="attachment_14701964" aria-describedby="caption-attachment-14701964" style="width: 1280px" class="wp-caption aligncenter"><a target="_blank" href="https://i.epochtimes.com/assets/uploads/2026/02/id14701964-1280_7.webp"><img loading="lazy" decoding="async" class="size-full wp-image-14701964" src="https://i.epochtimes.com/assets/uploads/2026/02/id14701964-1280_7.webp" alt="" width="1280" b="720" /></a><figcaption id="caption-attachment-14701964" class="wp-caption-text">卡匝伯尔嘲笑罗南是书呆子。（干净世界视频截图）</figcaption></figure>
<h2><strong>Sit around</strong></h2>
<p>Sit around 通常指随意坐着消磨时间，有时带有贬义，形容人懒散、不干活。Sit about 是英式表达。</p>
<p>Servant: Huh, why are you sitting around?<br />
女仆：啊，你为什么坐在那儿无所事事？</p>
<figure id="attachment_14701965" aria-describedby="caption-attachment-14701965" style="width: 1280px" class="wp-caption aligncenter"><a target="_blank" href="https://i.epochtimes.com/assets/uploads/2026/02/id14701965-1280_8.webp"><img loading="lazy" decoding="async" class="size-full wp-image-14701965" src="https://i.epochtimes.com/assets/uploads/2026/02/id14701965-1280_8.webp" alt="" width="1280" b="720" /></a><figcaption id="caption-attachment-14701965" class="wp-caption-text">仆人质疑罗南无所作为。（干净世界视频截图）</figcaption></figure>
<h2><strong>Hop on</strong></h2>
<p>Hop on 原意指跳上交通工具，也用来邀请别人加入自己，如上线打游戏、来聊天等。</p>
<p>Bjorn: Hop on and hold tight!<br />
熊爸爸：跳上我的背，抓紧喽！</p>
<figure id="attachment_14701966" aria-describedby="caption-attachment-14701966" style="width: 1280px" class="wp-caption aligncenter"><a target="_blank" href="https://i.epochtimes.com/assets/uploads/2026/02/id14701966-1280_9.webp"><img loading="lazy" decoding="async" class="size-full wp-image-14701966" src="https://i.epochtimes.com/assets/uploads/2026/02/id14701966-1280_9.webp" alt="" width="1280" b="720" /></a><figcaption id="caption-attachment-14701966" class="wp-caption-text">熊爸爸带罗南去找善良巫师芬恩。（干净世界视频截图）</figcaption></figure>
<p>在一些主要旅游城市可以看到一种敞篷双层观光巴士，可随上随下，即“hop-on hop-off”。这种观光巴士往往配有多语言语音导览服务，游客可凭24、48或72小时通票，在各主要景点的指定站自由上下车。下面是纽约随上随下观光巴士的介绍：</p>
<p>Explore New York City at your own pace with our 24-hour and 48-hour Hop-On Hop-Off Downtown &amp; Uptown Pass.<br />
在市中心和上城用我们提供的24小时和48小时的随上随下巴士通票，用自己的步调来探索纽约市。</p>
<h2><strong>Scaredy-cat</strong></h2>
<p>如果让谷歌来当翻译，“scaredy-cat”会被翻译成“吓人的猫”。因为猫容易受到惊吓，所以这个词的意思其实是“胆小鬼”、“怕死鬼”，是一种比较温和、可爱或带有戏谑意味的说法，常用于儿童或日常谈话中。</p>
<p>Come on, it&#8217;s just a spider! Don&#8217;t be such a scaredy-cat!<br />
来嘛，不过是只蜘蛛而已！别这么胆小鬼啦！</p>
<figure id="attachment_14701967" aria-describedby="caption-attachment-14701967" style="width: 1280px" class="wp-caption aligncenter"><a target="_blank" href="https://i.epochtimes.com/assets/uploads/2026/02/id14701967-1280_10.webp"><img loading="lazy" decoding="async" class="size-full wp-image-14701967" src="https://i.epochtimes.com/assets/uploads/2026/02/id14701967-1280_10.webp" alt="" width="1280" b="720" /></a><figcaption id="caption-attachment-14701967" class="wp-caption-text">熊爸爸不理解为何罗南不会打架。（干净世界视频截图）</figcaption></figure>
<p>另外，还有一些跟猫有关的习惯用语也很有趣，如：</p>
<p>Cool cat 形容时尚前卫的人，多指男性</p>
<p>Fat cat 肥猫、大亨、阔佬，常有贬义，指经营不善却领取高于一般行情的薪资与红利的企业高管，或吃公帑但不做事、甚至做坏事的公务员</p>
<p>Cat in the pan 叛徒、变节者</p>
<p>Cat&#8217;s paw 被人利用去火中取栗的牺牲品</p>
<p>As sick as a cat 病得厉害，尤其恶心想吐</p>
<p>Like a cat on hot bricks 像热锅上的蚂蚁</p>
<p>Cats and dogs 价值低的股票，但 rain cats and dogs 指滂沱大雨，而 agree like cats and dogs 则指合不来</p>
<p>Play cat and mouse. 欲擒故纵，忽冷忽热（猫抓到一些小动物之后，有时会故意松开爪子，等它们刚开始逃走时，却又逮住）。</p>
<p>When the cat’s away, the mice will play. 山中无老虎，猴子称大王。</p>
