<tr><td VALIGN=TOP width="626"><h2 align=center>组图：几十只白头鹰群聚同一棵树 罕见壮观</h2>
<img width="600" src="https://i.epochtimes.com/assets/uploads/2022/04/id13721537-et-eagles-6798-78965445-1200x845-600x400.jpg" />
<h6>上个月，明尼苏达州雷德温市的一位摄影师幸运拍到了数十只白头鹰栖息在密西西比河沿岸树枝上的壮观景象。（Tim Alms Photography提供）
</h6>
<hr>
	<p>【大纪元2022年04月27日讯】<span style="font-weight: 400;">（英文大纪元记者Michael Wing报导／张雨霏编译</span><span style="font-weight: 400;">）</span><span style="font-weight: 400;">过去如果能目击到一只<ahref="https://github.com/19920513/djy/blob/master/gb/tag/%E7%99%BD%E5%A4%B4%E9%B9%B0.md#1">白头鹰</a>都会引起不小的轰动，更不用说看到一群白头鹰栖息在同一棵树上了。因为近些年来，<ahref="https://github.com/19920513/djy/blob/master/gb/tag/%E7%BE%8E%E5%9B%BD%E5%9B%BD%E9%B8%9F.md#1">美国国鸟</a>白头鹰的种群数量出现断崖式下滑，甚至一度濒临灭绝。</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">然而，上个月，明尼苏达州雷德温市的摄影师蒂姆?阿尔姆斯（Tim Alms）却幸运地拍到了数十只<ahref="https://github.com/19920513/djy/blob/master/gb/tag/%E7%99%BD%E5%A4%B4%E9%B9%B0.md#1">白头鹰</a>（Bald Eagle，学名白头海雕）栖息在<ahref="https://github.com/19920513/djy/blob/master/gb/tag/%E5%AF%86%E8%A5%BF%E8%A5%BF%E6%AF%94%E6%B2%B3.md#1">密西西比河</a>沿岸树上的壮观景象。</span></p>
<figure id="attachment_13721535" aria-describedby="caption-attachment-13721535" style="width: 599px" class="wp-caption aligncenter"><a target="_blank" href="https://i.epochtimes.com/assets/uploads/2022/04/id13721535-Tim-Alms-Photography-1-1200x869.jpeg"><img class=" wp-image-13721535" src="https://i.epochtimes.com/assets/uploads/2022/04/id13721535-Tim-Alms-Photography-1-1200x869.jpeg" alt="" width="599" b="434" /></a><figcaption id="caption-attachment-13721535" class="wp-caption-text"><ahref="https://www.facebook.com/timalmsphotography/">Tim Alms Photography</a>提供）</figcaption></figure>
<figure id="attachment_13721536" aria-describedby="caption-attachment-13721536" style="width: 599px" class="wp-caption aligncenter"><a target="_blank" href="https://i.epochtimes.com/assets/uploads/2022/04/id13721536-et-eagles-6798-789-1200x833.jpg"><img class=" wp-image-13721536" src="https://i.epochtimes.com/assets/uploads/2022/04/id13721536-et-eagles-6798-789-1200x833.jpg" alt="" width="599" b="416" /></a><figcaption id="caption-attachment-13721536" class="wp-caption-text"><ahref="https://www.facebook.com/timalmsphotography/">Tim Alms Photography</a>提供）</figcaption></figure>
<figure id="attachment_13721538" aria-describedby="caption-attachment-13721538" style="width: 600px" class="wp-caption aligncenter"><a target="_blank" href="https://i.epochtimes.com/assets/uploads/2022/04/id13721538-et-eagles-6798-789543-1200x912.jpg"><img class=" wp-image-13721538" src="https://i.epochtimes.com/assets/uploads/2022/04/id13721538-et-eagles-6798-789543-1200x912.jpg" alt="" width="600" b="456" /></a><figcaption id="caption-attachment-13721538" class="wp-caption-text"><ahref="https://www.facebook.com/timalmsphotography/">Tim Alms Photography</a>提供）</figcaption></figure>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">这些威风凛凛的白头鹰之所以冬季会聚集在雷德温市的科维尔公园（Covill Park），因为这部分河段与<ahref="https://github.com/19920513/djy/blob/master/gb/tag/%E5%AF%86%E8%A5%BF%E8%A5%BF%E6%AF%94%E6%B2%B3.md#1">密西西比河</a>的其它部分不同，不会随着气温下降而结冰。再加上附近发电厂排出的再循环水使得该河流常年持续流淌，因此吸引了大批的白头鹰到这里来捕猎。</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">“这么多只鹰栖息在同一棵树上，我想很多人以前从未见过这种景象”，阿尔姆斯告诉福克斯新闻。</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">他还捕捉到了白头鹰以无与伦比的精确度捕猎的精彩瞬间，并与《大纪元时报》分享了这些珍贵的照片。画面显示，一只白头鹰急速向水面俯冲，用锋利的爪子抓起一条鱼，然后回到河边的一棵树上大快朵颐。</span></p>
<figure id="attachment_13721539" aria-describedby="caption-attachment-13721539" style="width: 600px" class="wp-caption aligncenter"><a target="_blank" href="https://i.epochtimes.com/assets/uploads/2022/04/id13721539-et-eagles-6798-78956-1200x801.jpg"><img class=" wp-image-13721539" src="https://i.epochtimes.com/assets/uploads/2022/04/id13721539-et-eagles-6798-78956-1200x801.jpg" alt="" width="600" b="401" /></a><figcaption id="caption-attachment-13721539" class="wp-caption-text"><ahref="https://www.facebook.com/timalmsphotography/">Tim Alms Photography</a>提供）</figcaption></figure>
<figure id="attachment_13721540" aria-describedby="caption-attachment-13721540" style="width: 599px" class="wp-caption aligncenter"><a target="_blank" href="https://i.epochtimes.com/assets/uploads/2022/04/id13721540-et-eagles-6798-78-1200x859.jpg"><img class=" wp-image-13721540" src="https://i.epochtimes.com/assets/uploads/2022/04/id13721540-et-eagles-6798-78-1200x859.jpg" alt="" width="599" b="429" /></a><figcaption id="caption-attachment-13721540" class="wp-caption-text"><ahref="https://www.facebook.com/timalmsphotography/">Tim Alms Photography</a>提供）</figcaption></figure>
<figure id="attachment_13721541" aria-describedby="caption-attachment-13721541" style="width: 600px" class="wp-caption aligncenter"><a target="_blank" href="https://i.epochtimes.com/assets/uploads/2022/04/id13721541-275939421_4950299528379703_3857973733108216854_n-1200x830.jpg"><img class=" wp-image-13721541" src="https://i.epochtimes.com/assets/uploads/2022/04/id13721541-275939421_4950299528379703_3857973733108216854_n-1200x830.jpg" alt="" width="600" b="415" /></a><figcaption id="caption-attachment-13721541" class="wp-caption-text"><ahref="https://www.facebook.com/timalmsphotography/">Tim Alms Photography</a>提供）</figcaption></figure>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">根据当地鹰类保护主义者、来自附近瓦巴沙市国家老鹰中心（National Eagle Center）的埃德?哈恩（Ed Hahn）的说法，随着天气变暖，这些鹰将向北迁徙，以便在春天筑巢。在接下来的几周内，它们就会迁徙，直到明年这个时候再返回来。</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">“它们聚集在一起的唯一原因，就像很多人不约而同去城里享用美味的自助餐一样”，哈恩说，在同一个地方看到如此多的猛禽在北美算得上是一件新鲜事，特别是因为这种雄伟的鸟类长期以来一直列在濒临灭绝的物种名单上。</span></p>
<figure id="attachment_13721542" aria-describedby="caption-attachment-13721542" style="width: 451px" class="wp-caption aligncenter"><a target="_blank" href="https://i.epochtimes.com/assets/uploads/2022/04/id13721542-et-eagles-6798-789678-1200x1532.jpg"><img class=" wp-image-13721542" src="https://i.epochtimes.com/assets/uploads/2022/04/id13721542-et-eagles-6798-789678-1200x1532.jpg" alt="" width="451" b="576" /></a><figcaption id="caption-attachment-13721542" class="wp-caption-text"><ahref="https://www.facebook.com/timalmsphotography/">Tim Alms Photography</a>提供）</figcaption></figure>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">“这些白头鹰真的可以称为珍贵的遗产，因为我们差点失去该物种，好在我们没有真的失去”，哈恩补充道，“今天它们正蓬勃发展，我们需要将这些经验应用到其它需要我们帮助的动物身上。北美的白头鹰是我们在野生动物保护方面取得的最大成功案例。”</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">白头鹰是北美独有的鹰种，在野外的寿命可以长达30多年，它们配对生活，从一而终。同一对鹰往往会年复一年地回到同一个窝巢，并不断加建，经历多个季节后，庞大的尺寸成为它们感情坚贞的象征。</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">美国于1782年将白头鹰定为国鸟。18世纪，全国估计有10万对以上，但由于长期捕杀、失去栖息地、水质下降和化学药品如DDT的使用等原因，到了1960年代，其种群数量不足1000只。后来经过长期的保护和努力，目前数量已经恢复到超过31万只，从几近灭绝的状态中被挽救出来。◇</span></p>
<figure id="attachment_13721543" aria-describedby="caption-attachment-13721543" style="width: 600px" class="wp-caption aligncenter"><a target="_blank" href="https://i.epochtimes.com/assets/uploads/2022/04/id13721543-et-eagles-6798-7894-1200x813.jpg"><img class=" wp-image-13721543" src="https://i.epochtimes.com/assets/uploads/2022/04/id13721543-et-eagles-6798-7894-1200x813.jpg" alt="" width="600" b="407" /></a><figcaption id="caption-attachment-13721543" class="wp-caption-text"><ahref="https://www.facebook.com/timalmsphotography/">Tim Alms Photography</a>提供）</figcaption></figure>
