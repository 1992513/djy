<tr><td VALIGN=TOP width="626"><h2 align=center>【旅行趣】快闪克罗埃西亚最古老的城市─史普利特</h2>
<img width="600" src="https://i.epochtimes.com/assets/uploads/2024/11/id14366328-20241107_split_croatia_01-600x400.jpg" />
<h6></h6>
<hr>
	<p>&nbsp;</p>
<figure id="attachment_14366300" aria-describedby="caption-attachment-14366300" style="width: 600px" class="wp-caption aligncenter"><a target="_blank" href="https://i.epochtimes.com/assets/uploads/2024/11/id14366300-8e9be6b858ce41d296754290b86fd0c5.jpg"><img decoding="async" class="size-large wp-image-14366300" src="https://i.epochtimes.com/assets/uploads/2024/11/id14366300-8e9be6b858ce41d296754290b86fd0c5-600x544.jpg" alt="" width="600" b="544" /></a><figcaption id="caption-attachment-14366300" class="wp-caption-text">街上常能见到装扮成罗马士兵的街头艺人。（图／作者提供）</figcaption></figure>
<p>史普利特（Split）是克罗埃西亚（Croatia）的第二大城，有着超过1700年的历史，是一座拥有中世纪遗迹的港都，也是克罗埃西亚最古老的城市。因曾被罗马帝国统治，所以街上常能见到装扮成罗马士兵的街头艺人，在古迹建筑前停下和旅客合照索费。光是看这些罗马士兵自在的穿梭大街小巷，便让旅人有种时空穿越的奇妙感受。</p>
<figure id="attachment_14366294" aria-describedby="caption-attachment-14366294" style="width: 600px" class="wp-caption aligncenter"><a target="_blank" href="https://i.epochtimes.com/assets/uploads/2024/11/id14366294-442bb95ae20cbe0c40e7f4fe9e43a7b2.jpg"><img decoding="async" class="size-large wp-image-14366294" src="https://i.epochtimes.com/assets/uploads/2024/11/id14366294-442bb95ae20cbe0c40e7f4fe9e43a7b2-600x450.jpg" alt="" width="600" b="450" /></a><figcaption id="caption-attachment-14366294" class="wp-caption-text">“史普利特主教座堂”外的廊柱中庭。 （图／作者提供）</figcaption></figure>
<p>史普利特旧城区有一座中世纪遗留下的八角形建筑，即列为世界文化遗产的“史普利特主教座堂”（Katedrala Sv. Duje，以下简称大教堂）。大教堂旁矗立着高约60公尺的钟楼，虽然内部楼梯狭窄陡峭，但因爬上钟楼就能环视史普利特旧城区、亚得里亚海（Adriatic Sea）及远处的山景。因此造访大教堂的旅客，绝大部分都满心欢喜的想爬上钟楼一探究竟！</p>
<figure id="attachment_14366295" aria-describedby="caption-attachment-14366295" style="width: 450px" class="wp-caption aligncenter"><a target="_blank" href="https://i.epochtimes.com/assets/uploads/2024/11/id14366295-b0181df119f6514d8a7d9dc9849195f8.jpg"><img decoding="async" class=" wp-image-14366295" src="https://i.epochtimes.com/assets/uploads/2024/11/id14366295-b0181df119f6514d8a7d9dc9849195f8-600x800.jpg" alt="" width="450" b="600" /></a><figcaption id="caption-attachment-14366295" class="wp-caption-text">60公尺篓空的中世纪钟楼，惧高症者切莫轻易尝试！（图／作者提供）</figcaption></figure>
<figure id="attachment_14366302" aria-describedby="caption-attachment-14366302" style="width: 450px" class="wp-caption aligncenter"><a target="_blank" href="https://i.epochtimes.com/assets/uploads/2024/11/id14366302-18540f0497bebba1029865faff3bc226.jpg"><img decoding="async" class=" wp-image-14366302" src="https://i.epochtimes.com/assets/uploads/2024/11/id14366302-18540f0497bebba1029865faff3bc226-600x737.jpg" alt="" width="450" b="553" /></a><figcaption id="caption-attachment-14366302" class="wp-caption-text">胆大的旅人，很推荐来钟楼体验一番！（图／作者提供）</figcaption></figure>
<p>我没有惧高症，但爬上60公尺（相当于18层楼高）且篓空的钟楼，双脚像踩在云朵上，心悬在半空中，越往上爬心跳越快速。奉劝大家不要轻易往下看，否则容易感到呼吸困难、两脚发软，甚至一度天旋地转，有点像小老鼠上灯台，偷吃油下不来，陷入不知该咬牙往上攀登，还是缓步下行的困窘感。真心奉劝惧高症者，请勿轻易尝试！至于胆大的旅人，很推荐来钟楼体验一番，其刺激感对我而言，就像坐了好几趟海盗船！</p>
<figure id="attachment_14366297" aria-describedby="caption-attachment-14366297" style="width: 450px" class="wp-caption aligncenter"><a target="_blank" href="https://i.epochtimes.com/assets/uploads/2024/11/id14366297-65a63fa885b9a6561d59dfa59da5ab1b.jpg"><img decoding="async" class=" wp-image-14366297" src="https://i.epochtimes.com/assets/uploads/2024/11/id14366297-65a63fa885b9a6561d59dfa59da5ab1b-600x800.jpg" alt="" width="450" b="600" /></a><figcaption id="caption-attachment-14366297" class="wp-caption-text">爬上钟楼，就能环视史普利特旧城区。（图／作者提供）</figcaption></figure>
<figure id="attachment_14366298" aria-describedby="caption-attachment-14366298" style="width: 600px" class="wp-caption aligncenter"><a target="_blank" href="https://i.epochtimes.com/assets/uploads/2024/11/id14366298-249d973dd7113c64c41d196065b45995.jpg"><img decoding="async" class="size-large wp-image-14366298" src="https://i.epochtimes.com/assets/uploads/2024/11/id14366298-249d973dd7113c64c41d196065b45995-600x450.jpg" alt="" width="600" b="450" /></a><figcaption id="caption-attachment-14366298" class="wp-caption-text">从大教堂的钟楼俯瞰史普利特旧城区。（图／作者提供）</figcaption></figure>
<p>另外值得一提的是，庄严的大教堂外，竟是悠闲的露天餐厅，两者相互交织成一幅有趣的画面。傍晚时分暑气渐散，不妨停下脚步，在古罗马式廊柱的陪伴下，享受一顿丰盛的海鲜大餐，犒赏自己一路旅途的辛劳。</p>
<figure id="attachment_14366303" aria-describedby="caption-attachment-14366303" style="width: 600px" class="wp-caption aligncenter"><a target="_blank" href="https://i.epochtimes.com/assets/uploads/2024/11/id14366303-5278455b5a822ea4e325fc0efed77885.jpg"><img decoding="async" class="size-large wp-image-14366303" src="https://i.epochtimes.com/assets/uploads/2024/11/id14366303-5278455b5a822ea4e325fc0efed77885-600x450.jpg" alt="" width="600" b="450" /></a><figcaption id="caption-attachment-14366303" class="wp-caption-text">露天餐厅和庄严的教堂，形成有趣的交叠。（图／作者提供）</figcaption></figure>
<figure id="attachment_14366296" aria-describedby="caption-attachment-14366296" style="width: 600px" class="wp-caption aligncenter"><a target="_blank" href="https://i.epochtimes.com/assets/uploads/2024/11/id14366296-d39ac905c1aec48b868ea8e0c136acef.jpg"><img decoding="async" class="size-large wp-image-14366296" src="https://i.epochtimes.com/assets/uploads/2024/11/id14366296-d39ac905c1aec48b868ea8e0c136acef-600x450.jpg" alt="" width="600" b="450" /></a><figcaption id="caption-attachment-14366296" class="wp-caption-text">在大教堂外的露天餐厅享受海鲜大餐。（图／作者提供）</figcaption></figure>
<p>史普利特旧城区还有一处圆形空间的前庭，由于前庭的筒状结构能使声音发挥更佳效果，因此常有男合唱团在此高歌。他们的音乐是一种称为Klapa的传统克罗埃西亚无伴奏合唱曲，有时也会贩售CD，喜欢的话不妨惠顾珍藏。动听的乐曲，总让人回忆起旅途中的种种美好，不是吗？</p>
<figure id="attachment_14366299" aria-describedby="caption-attachment-14366299" style="width: 600px" class="wp-caption aligncenter"><a target="_blank" href="https://i.epochtimes.com/assets/uploads/2024/11/id14366299-94dbd138558e7fb02e8ac21dbeac752e.jpg"><img decoding="async" class="size-large wp-image-14366299" src="https://i.epochtimes.com/assets/uploads/2024/11/id14366299-94dbd138558e7fb02e8ac21dbeac752e-600x529.jpg" alt="" width="600" b="529" /></a><figcaption id="caption-attachment-14366299" class="wp-caption-text">无伴奏男声合唱团体，常在回音效果良好的厅廊演唱。（图／作者提供）</figcaption></figure>
<p>史普利特四周有市墙环绕，北东南西边都设有一座大门，分别是“金之门”、“银之门”、“铜之门”、“铁之门”。其中“金之门”旁的宁斯基主教雕像 （Statue of Bishop Gregory of Nin），据说碰碰他巨大的左脚姆指，就会带来好运，所以主教的脚拇指被摸得亮晶晶。就像台湾庙宇里的“石来运转”，常被摸得发亮。原来祈求好运的仪式感，似乎东西方皆然！史普利特古城快闪，下一站，前进亚得里亚海诸岛！＠＊</p>
<figure id="attachment_14366301" aria-describedby="caption-attachment-14366301" style="width: 450px" class="wp-caption aligncenter"><a target="_blank" href="https://i.epochtimes.com/assets/uploads/2024/11/id14366301-7f7ad61cb9929d220f1af38f07f4b140.jpg"><img decoding="async" class=" wp-image-14366301" src="https://i.epochtimes.com/assets/uploads/2024/11/id14366301-7f7ad61cb9929d220f1af38f07f4b140-600x584.jpg" alt="" width="450" b="438" /></a><figcaption id="caption-attachment-14366301" class="wp-caption-text">宁斯基主教雕像（Statue of Bishop Gregory of Nin）。据说碰他的左脚姆指，会带来好运！（图／作者提供）</figcaption></figure>
