<tr><td VALIGN=TOP width="626"><h2 align=center>一家人将120年历史老火车厢改成豪华旅馆</h2>
<img width="600" src="https://i.epochtimes.com/assets/uploads/2024/09/id14330824-TrainThumb-1080x720-1-600x400.jpg" />
<h6>爱达荷州一家人将120年历史的腐坏火车车厢翻修成豪华旅馆。此为火车车厢改装前后的对比图。（由《大纪元时报》和Isaac French提供）
</h6>
<hr>
	<p>【大纪元2024年09月23日讯】（英文大纪元记者Tyler Wilson和Daksha Devnani报导／赵孜济编译）几年前的冬天，爱达荷州的爱德华?弗兰奇（Edward French）在帮助他的老邻居铲掉屋顶上的积雪时，偶然发现了一节埋在谷仓里的几乎无法辨认、野猫出没的火车车厢。</p>
<p>这位55岁的男子还发现了一张摄于1906年的火车车厢的照片。他开始与当地人谈论它的历史，这激起了他的兴趣。他发现306号火车车厢曾经在华盛顿和蒙大拿州被频繁地使用，直到1955年。停运后，一位农民将其拖到10英里外的田地，用作谷物和干草仓库。</p>
<p>几十年来，这个已有120年历史的老古董一直湮没在农场上，最终成为一道过时的风景。</p>
<figure style="width: 600px" class="wp-caption aligncenter"><ahref=" https://www.theepochtimes.com/_next/image?url=https%3A%2F%2Fimg.theepochtimes.com%2Fassets%2Fuploads%2F2024%2F08%2F23%2Fid5711942-120-Year-Old-Train-Car-Rennovation-_5.jpeg&amp;w=1200&amp;q=75" target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener"> <img decoding="async" class="" src="https://www.theepochtimes.com/_next/image?url=https%3A%2F%2Fimg.theepochtimes.com%2Fassets%2Fuploads%2F2024%2F08%2F23%2Fid5711942-120-Year-Old-Train-Car-Rennovation-_5.jpeg&amp;w=1200&amp;q=75" width="600" b="487" /></a><figcaption class="wp-caption-text">这辆有120年历史的火车残骸是在爱达荷州的一个农场发现的。（由Isaac French提供）</figcaption></figure>
<p>尽管气味难闻，但经营一家建筑公司的爱德华看到了腐烂和废墟之外的东西。他仅花2,000美元就买下了这个被忽视的历史遗迹，并计划修复它。</p>
<p>在爱达荷州寒冷的冬天，爱德华和他的八个儿子将这辆61英尺长的车厢搬到了他145英亩的土地上。他在2020年3月至8月致力于这项工程。他们投资了14.7万美元和无数工时，将其恢复到昔日的辉煌——而且更漂亮。</p>
<figure style="width: 601px" class="wp-caption aligncenter"><ahref=" https://www.theepochtimes.com/_next/image?url=https%3A%2F%2Fimg.theepochtimes.com%2Fassets%2Fuploads%2F2024%2F08%2F23%2Fid5711945-120-Year-Old-Train-Car-Rennovation-_8.jpeg&amp;w=1200&amp;q=75" target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener"> <img decoding="async" class="" src="https://www.theepochtimes.com/_next/image?url=https%3A%2F%2Fimg.theepochtimes.com%2Fassets%2Fuploads%2F2024%2F08%2F23%2Fid5711945-120-Year-Old-Train-Car-Rennovation-_8.jpeg&amp;w=1200&amp;q=75" width="601" b="355" /></a><figcaption class="wp-caption-text">火车车厢沿着蜿蜒的丘陵道路驶向新家。（由Isaac French提供）</figcaption></figure>
<figure style="width: 601px" class="wp-caption aligncenter"><ahref=" https://www.theepochtimes.com/_next/image?url=https%3A%2F%2Fimg.theepochtimes.com%2Fassets%2Fuploads%2F2024%2F08%2F23%2Fid5711937-120-Year-Old-Train-Car-Rennovation-_1.jpeg&amp;w=1200&amp;q=75" target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener"> <img decoding="async" class="" src="https://www.theepochtimes.com/_next/image?url=https%3A%2F%2Fimg.theepochtimes.com%2Fassets%2Fuploads%2F2024%2F08%2F23%2Fid5711937-120-Year-Old-Train-Car-Rennovation-_1.jpeg&amp;w=1200&amp;q=75" width="601" b="434" /></a><figcaption class="wp-caption-text">（由Isaac French提供）</figcaption></figure>
<p>首先，爱德华和他的儿子们把木头剥了下来。为了将其全部打磨干净，他们甚至使用牙签和牙刷将所有缝隙清理干净，然后他们发现车厢的原始结构实际上状况良好。</p>
<p>随着原始车身的核心开始浮现，这家人意识到它可以恢复昔日的辉煌。</p>
<p>“我认为，这首先只是那种从无到有（zero to one）的信念，它实际上是值得救赎的。”爱德华的儿子艾萨克?弗兰奇（Isaac French）告诉《大纪元时报》，“我们走得越远，就越令人鼓舞，因为我们又开始小心翼翼地刮掉腐坏的地方，找到了原来的手绘字母——306。”</p>
<figure style="width: 599px" class="wp-caption aligncenter"><ahref=" https://www.theepochtimes.com/_next/image?url=https%3A%2F%2Fimg.theepochtimes.com%2Fassets%2Fuploads%2F2024%2F08%2F23%2Fid5711943-120-Year-Old-Train-Car-Rennovation-_6.jpeg&amp;w=1200&amp;q=75" target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener"> <img decoding="async" class="" src="https://www.theepochtimes.com/_next/image?url=https%3A%2F%2Fimg.theepochtimes.com%2Fassets%2Fuploads%2F2024%2F08%2F23%2Fid5711943-120-Year-Old-Train-Car-Rennovation-_6.jpeg&amp;w=1200&amp;q=75" width="599" b="449" /></a><figcaption class="wp-caption-text">（由Isaac French提供）</figcaption></figure>
<figure style="width: 450px" class="wp-caption aligncenter"><ahref=" https://www.theepochtimes.com/_next/image?url=https%3A%2F%2Fimg.theepochtimes.com%2Fassets%2Fuploads%2F2024%2F08%2F23%2Fid5711944-120-Year-Old-Train-Car-Rennovation-_7.jpeg&amp;w=1200&amp;q=75" target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener"> <img decoding="async" class="" src="https://www.theepochtimes.com/_next/image?url=https%3A%2F%2Fimg.theepochtimes.com%2Fassets%2Fuploads%2F2024%2F08%2F23%2Fid5711944-120-Year-Old-Train-Car-Rennovation-_7.jpeg&amp;w=1200&amp;q=75" width="450" b="600" /></a><figcaption class="wp-caption-text">翻新前的火车车厢内部。（由Isaac French提供）</figcaption></figure>
<figure style="width: 601px" class="wp-caption aligncenter"><ahref=" https://www.theepochtimes.com/_next/image?url=https%3A%2F%2Fimg.theepochtimes.com%2Fassets%2Fuploads%2F2024%2F08%2F23%2Fid5711949-120-Year-Old-Train-Car-Rennovation-_11.jpeg&amp;w=1200&amp;q=75" target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener"> <img decoding="async" class="" src="https://www.theepochtimes.com/_next/image?url=https%3A%2F%2Fimg.theepochtimes.com%2Fassets%2Fuploads%2F2024%2F08%2F23%2Fid5711949-120-Year-Old-Train-Car-Rennovation-_11.jpeg&amp;w=1200&amp;q=75" width="601" b="451" /></a><figcaption class="wp-caption-text">（由Isaac French提供）</figcaption></figure>
<p>这个数字的发现证实了爱德华找到的就是原始照片中的火车。</p>
<p>“然后我们把这些线索连接起来，我们确切地知道它的历史是什么。”在德克萨斯州从事酒店顾问工作的艾萨克说。</p>
<p>“就像在任何修复项目中一样，你都会发现一些你认为不会存在的东西。”他说，“对我们来说，那是太腐烂的部分，所以我们不得不小心翼翼地用新木片进行修补。但就所有材料而言，其中90%到95%都是原装的。”</p>
<figure style="width: 450px" class="wp-caption aligncenter"><ahref=" https://www.theepochtimes.com/_next/image?url=https%3A%2F%2Fimg.theepochtimes.com%2Fassets%2Fuploads%2F2024%2F08%2F23%2Fid5711954-120-Year-Old-Train-Car-Rennovation-_15.jpeg&amp;w=1200&amp;q=75" target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener"> <img decoding="async" class="" src="https://www.theepochtimes.com/_next/image?url=https%3A%2F%2Fimg.theepochtimes.com%2Fassets%2Fuploads%2F2024%2F08%2F23%2Fid5711954-120-Year-Old-Train-Car-Rennovation-_15.jpeg&amp;w=1200&amp;q=75" width="450" b="600" /></a><figcaption class="wp-caption-text">（由Isaac French提供）</figcaption></figure>
<figure style="width: 601px" class="wp-caption aligncenter"><ahref=" https://www.theepochtimes.com/_next/image?url=https%3A%2F%2Fimg.theepochtimes.com%2Fassets%2Fuploads%2F2024%2F08%2F23%2Fid5711955-120-Year-Old-Train-Car-Rennovation-_16.jpeg&amp;w=1200&amp;q=75" target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener"> <img decoding="async" class="" src="https://www.theepochtimes.com/_next/image?url=https%3A%2F%2Fimg.theepochtimes.com%2Fassets%2Fuploads%2F2024%2F08%2F23%2Fid5711955-120-Year-Old-Train-Car-Rennovation-_16.jpeg&amp;w=1200&amp;q=75" width="601" b="451" /></a><figcaption class="wp-caption-text">弗兰奇一家共同努力改造了火车车厢。（由Isaac French提供）</figcaption></figure>
<figure style="width: 600px" class="wp-caption aligncenter"><ahref=" https://www.theepochtimes.com/_next/image?url=https%3A%2F%2Fimg.theepochtimes.com%2Fassets%2Fuploads%2F2024%2F08%2F23%2Fid5711941-120-Year-Old-Train-Car-Rennovation-_4.jpeg&amp;w=1200&amp;q=75" target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener"> <img decoding="async" class="" src="https://www.theepochtimes.com/_next/image?url=https%3A%2F%2Fimg.theepochtimes.com%2Fassets%2Fuploads%2F2024%2F08%2F23%2Fid5711941-120-Year-Old-Train-Car-Rennovation-_4.jpeg&amp;w=1200&amp;q=75" width="600" b="450" /></a><figcaption class="wp-caption-text">（由Isaac French提供）</figcaption></figure>
<p>艾萨克和他的手足们不懈地努力修复火车车厢的每一部分，为最终成为他们宾馆的这个空间注入了新的活力。</p>
<p>“邮件厢变成了卧室，货物区变成了浴室，乘客区变成了小厨房和休息室。”艾萨克在X（前称Twitter）上的一篇现已广泛传播的帖子中写道。</p>
<p>艾萨克的一位哥哥还在围绕车厢建造了一个木结构棚子和平台，屋顶线条模仿了传统的车辆段建筑。</p>
<p>为了与1900年代的原始设计保持一致，弗兰奇一家使用了传统的油漆颜色、手工制作的灯具和那个时代的家具。</p>
<p>“（这）就像是回到了过去，回到了作为乘客乘坐那个车厢的感觉。”艾萨克说。</p>
<figure style="width: 450px" class="wp-caption aligncenter"><ahref=" https://www.theepochtimes.com/_next/image?url=https%3A%2F%2Fimg.theepochtimes.com%2Fassets%2Fuploads%2F2024%2F08%2F23%2Fid5711956-120-Year-Old-Train-Car-Rennovation-_17.jpeg&amp;w=1200&amp;q=75" target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener"> <img decoding="async" class="" src="https://www.theepochtimes.com/_next/image?url=https%3A%2F%2Fimg.theepochtimes.com%2Fassets%2Fuploads%2F2024%2F08%2F23%2Fid5711956-120-Year-Old-Train-Car-Rennovation-_17.jpeg&amp;w=1200&amp;q=75" width="450" b="600" /></a><figcaption class="wp-caption-text">翻新后的火车车厢内部。（由Isaac French提供）</figcaption></figure>
<p>通过修复过程，艾萨克和他的手足们——他们在家接受教育，并在成长过程中在农场工作——彼此加深了联系。</p>
<p>“当世界上这么多地方都关闭时，能进行这样的活动真是太棒了。”他说，并补充说，“能够为这样一段历史注入活力，这是非常有意义的。”</p>
<p>完成项目并迎来他们的第一位房客，这对弗兰奇一家来说是一件令人欣慰的事情。</p>
<figure style="width: 450px" class="wp-caption aligncenter"><ahref=" https://www.theepochtimes.com/_next/image?url=https%3A%2F%2Fimg.theepochtimes.com%2Fassets%2Fuploads%2F2024%2F08%2F23%2Fid5711957-Issac_family-photo.jpeg&amp;w=1200&amp;q=75" target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener"> <img decoding="async" class="" src="https://www.theepochtimes.com/_next/image?url=https%3A%2F%2Fimg.theepochtimes.com%2Fassets%2Fuploads%2F2024%2F08%2F23%2Fid5711957-Issac_family-photo.jpeg&amp;w=1200&amp;q=75" width="450" b="629" /></a><figcaption class="wp-caption-text">弗兰奇一家。（由Isaac French提供）</figcaption></figure>
<p>艾萨克于当年10月结婚，大部分蜜月时间都在新英格兰度过。他回到爱达荷州后，与妻子在火车车厢里度过了几个晚上。</p>
<p>他说，那一刻让他回味无穷。</p>
<p>“能从那个角度去体验它，真的很有成就感，因为我们为此付出了很多努力。”他说。</p>
<figure style="width: 600px" class="wp-caption aligncenter"><ahref=" https://www.theepochtimes.com/_next/image?url=https%3A%2F%2Fimg.theepochtimes.com%2Fassets%2Fuploads%2F2024%2F08%2F23%2Fid5711950-120-Year-Old-Train-Car-Rennovation-_12.jpeg&amp;w=1200&amp;q=75" target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener"> <img decoding="async" class="" src="https://www.theepochtimes.com/_next/image?url=https%3A%2F%2Fimg.theepochtimes.com%2Fassets%2Fuploads%2F2024%2F08%2F23%2Fid5711950-120-Year-Old-Train-Car-Rennovation-_12.jpeg&amp;w=1200&amp;q=75" width="600" b="353" /></a><figcaption class="wp-caption-text">翻新后的外观。（由Isaac French提供）</figcaption></figure>
<p>当《大纪元时报》问艾萨克他最喜欢火车上的哪个地方时，他说是那个小休息区。</p>
<p>“在那里的感觉真是太舒适了。这是一个坐下来阅读或欣赏风景的好地方”，他说，“显然，车厢在景观上的位置令人惊叹。”</p>
<p>艾萨克说，火车车厢坐落在山顶上，农田和森林的景色“非常壮观”。</p>
